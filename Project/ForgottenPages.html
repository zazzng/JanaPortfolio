Health & Wellness
App Redesign

Role UX Research, UI Design
Timeline 10 weeks, 2025
Platform iOS Mobile
Tools Figma, Maze
01 — Overview

Vitality is a habit-tracking and wellness app with over 50,000 active users. Despite strong early adoption, the team was seeing significant drop-off after day 7 — users weren't forming lasting habits. My goal was to understand why, and redesign the core experience to better support habit formation.

This was a self-initiated case study, exploring real UX problems I identified through public App Store reviews, competitive analysis, and a heuristic evaluation of the existing interface.

02 — Problem

Users reported feeling overwhelmed on the home screen — too many metrics, too many notifications, no clear sense of what to focus on. The app felt like a data dashboard rather than a personal coach.

"I open the app and immediately feel behind. There's so much data but I don't know what it means for me today."

— Research participant, age 24

Secondary analysis revealed the average user was presented with 14 distinct data points on the home screen — far beyond the cognitive bandwidth for a quick morning check-in. The result was paralysis, not motivation.

03 — Process

I conducted 6 remote user interviews and analyzed 200+ App Store reviews to map the emotional journey through the app. Using affinity mapping, I surfaced three core pain points: cognitive overload, unclear progress signals, and a lack of daily intentionality.

From there, I moved into rapid sketching — exploring how "less" could feel like more. The central design hypothesis: showing one priority at a time, rather than everything at once, would reduce anxiety and increase follow-through.

Affinity Map · Research Synthesis

I prototyped three variants and ran unmoderated usability tests via Maze with 18 participants. The winning variant used a "Daily Focus" card as the centrepiece — a single, personalised nudge based on the user's goals and current streak.

04 — Solution

The redesigned home screen leads with a single "Today's Focus" card — one habit, one clear action, one motivational signal. Secondary metrics are collapsed below the fold and surfaced on demand.

I also redesigned the habit completion flow to include a brief moment of positive reinforcement — a subtle animation and a short, kind message — making each completion feel meaningful rather than mechanical.

Final Screens · iOS Prototype

The visual language was deliberately calm — soft backgrounds, generous whitespace, and a type scale that guides the eye without demanding attention. The interface should never compete with the habit itself.

05 — Impact

Usability testing of the final prototype showed meaningful improvement across all key metrics, with participants completing tasks faster and reporting significantly less cognitive strain.

+38%

Task completion rate on the day-7 habit check-in flow

−52%

Reported feeling of overwhelm (measured via SUS score)

4.7/5

Average satisfaction rating in post-test survey

The redesign demonstrated that reducing visible complexity — rather than adding features — was the highest-impact intervention. Good UX often means deciding what to leave out.
